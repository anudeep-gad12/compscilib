Median Absolute Deviation (MAD)

The Median Absolute Deviation (MAD) is a measure of statistical dispersion. It is the median of the absolute deviations from the data's median. This makes it a robust measure of the variability of a dataset.

Input: 3, 6, 7, 8, 8, 10, 15, 16, 20

Step 1: Calculate the Median of the Dataset
  • Organize the data from smallest to largest and find the central value.
  • Sorted data: (3, 6, 7, 8, 8, 10, 15, 16, 20)
  • Determine the number of observations in your dataset. This count (let's call it N) is important to figure out whether you have an odd or even number of data points. \[N=9\]
  • For an Odd Number of Data Points (N is odd): The median is the value exactly in the middle of the ordered list. \[\frac{N + 1}{2}\]
  • For an Even Number of Data Points (N is even): The median is the average of the two middle values. To find these values, use the positions \[\frac{N}{2}\ , \frac{N}{2} + 1\]

Here the number of observations are odd. Hence the median is 8.

Step 2: Compute Absolute Deviations from the Median

Determine the absolute differences between the median and each data point.

  • Go through each data point in your dataset. For a data point x, calculate its absolute deviation from the median M using the formula:
  • Absolute Deviation: \[|x - M|\] here vertical bars |...| denote the absolute value, ensuring the result is non-negative.
\[ \begin{aligned} |3 - 8| &= 5, & |6 - 8| &= 2, & |7 - 8| &= 1, \\|8 - 8| &= 0, & |8 - 8| &= 0, & |10 - 8| &= 2, \\ |15 - 8| &= 7, &|16 - 8| &= 8, & |20 - 8| &= 12 \end{aligned} \]
  • The new data set is (5, 2, 1, 0, 0, 2, 7, 8, 12)
Step 3: Calculate the Median of the Absolute Deviations

Organize the absolute deviations and find their median. This is the MAD.

  • Sort the Absolute Deviations:
    Ordered list: (0, 0, 1, 2, 2, 5, 7, 8, 12)
  • As calculated the median find the median for this new dataset
  • The MAD is the middle value of the ordered list of absolute deviations (5th value) \[(9 + 1) / 2 = 5\].
  • The 5th value in the ordered list is 2.

Answer:

The Median Absolute Deviation (MAD) of the dataset is 2.

This indicates that the typical deviation from the median of the dataset is 2 units.
